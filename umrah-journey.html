The Lesser Pilgrimage

The Umrah Journey

A blessed journey that can be performed at any time of the year. Experience the spiritual renewal of visiting Allah's Sacred House through our immersive VR platform.

What is Umrah?

Umrah is the "lesser pilgrimage" to Makkah that can be performed at any time of the year, unlike Hajj which has specific dates. While not obligatory, Umrah is highly recommended (Sunnah Mu'akkadah) and carries immense spiritual rewards. It is a chance to seek closeness to Allah, seek forgiveness, and renew one's faith.

وَأَتِمُّوا الْحَجَّ وَالْعُمْرَةَ لِلَّهِ

"And complete the Hajj and Umrah for Allah."

— Quran 2:196

Expiation of Sins

The Prophet ﷺ said: "One Umrah to the next is an expiation for what is between them."

Any Time of Year

Unlike Hajj, Umrah can be performed throughout the year, making it more accessible.

Shorter Duration

Umrah can be completed in a few hours, making it ideal for those with limited time.

Spiritual Renewal

A chance to reconnect with Allah, refresh your iman, and return spiritually recharged.

The Sacred Rituals

Steps of Umrah

Four essential rituals to complete your Umrah

01

Ihram

Enter the sacred state at the Miqat (designated boundary). Perform ghusl (ritual bath), wear Ihram garments, make your intention, and begin reciting the Talbiyah.

Intention for Umrah:

اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أُرِيدُ الْعُمْرَةَ فَيَسِّرْهَا لِي وَتَقَبَّلْهَا مِنِّي

"O Allah, I intend to perform Umrah, so make it easy for me and accept it from me."

Ihram Restrictions:

  • No cutting hair or nails
  • No perfume or scented products
  • No covering the head (men)
  • No hunting or harming animals
  • No marital relations
02

Tawaf

Circle the Kaaba seven times in a counter-clockwise direction, starting from the Black Stone (Hajr al-Aswad). Each circuit is called a "shawt." Men should perform Raml (brisk walking) in the first three circuits.

At the Black Stone:

بِسْمِ اللهِ، اللهُ أَكْبَرُ

"In the name of Allah, Allah is the Greatest"

Between Rukn Yamani and Black Stone:

رَبَّنَا آتِنَا فِي الدُّنْيَا حَسَنَةً وَفِي الْآخِرَةِ حَسَنَةً وَقِنَا عَذَابَ النَّارِ

"Our Lord, give us good in this world and good in the Hereafter, and protect us from the punishment of the Fire."

After Tawaf, pray 2 rakats behind Maqam Ibrahim if possible
03

Sa'i

Walk seven times between the hills of Safa and Marwa, commemorating Hajar's desperate search for water for her infant son Ismail. Start at Safa and end at Marwa. The total distance is about 3.15 km.

Upon reaching Safa:

إِنَّ الصَّفَا وَالْمَرْوَةَ مِنْ شَعَائِرِ اللهِ

"Indeed, Safa and Marwa are among the symbols of Allah."

Safa → Marwa = 1 lap | 7 laps total
04

Halq or Taqsir

Complete your Umrah by shaving the head (Halq - recommended for men) or trimming the hair (Taqsir). Women cut a fingertip's length from the ends of their hair. This releases you from the state of Ihram.

Umrah Maqbool! Your Umrah is now complete. May Allah accept it.
Enhance Your Experience

Recommended Acts

Drink Zamzam Water

Drink from the blessed Zamzam well and make dua. The Prophet ﷺ said: "The water of Zamzam is for whatever purpose it is drunk for."

Pray at Maqam Ibrahim

After Tawaf, pray 2 rakats behind Maqam Ibrahim if possible, or anywhere in the Masjid.

Gaze at the Kaaba

Looking at the Kaaba is an act of worship. Take moments to simply gaze and make heartfelt dua.

Recite Quran

Spend time reciting Quran in the sacred Masjid where rewards are multiplied manifold.

Night Prayers

Pray Tahajjud and spend the blessed nights in worship at the Sacred Mosque.

Visit Madinah

If possible, visit Madinah to pray at Masjid an-Nabawi and send salutations upon the Prophet ﷺ.
